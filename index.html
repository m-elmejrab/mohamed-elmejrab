Mohamed Elmejrab
Multidisciplinary artist with diverse works in media and video games

Mohamed Elmejrab is a creative professional working across TV production, post-production, and game development. With a focus on meaningful, engaging content, he has contributed to a range of Libyan media and game projects that reached wide audiences and sparked positive conversations.

As a Production Manager, Mohamed led the production of Marionette, a Libyan TV drama broadcast on Libya Al-Ahrar TV. He managed a cast and crew of over 55 people and successfully delivered all 10 episodes on time and within budget. The series was watched by millions both on television and online. He also managed the award-winning COVID-19 awareness video A New Reality, which won RNW Media’s Best COVID-19 Awareness Video award in 2020, bringing together healthcare workers and citizens in a powerful call to action.

As a Colorist, Mohamed worked on Masaha, a unique talk show promoting social dialogue, and Alash?! Studio, a bold program tackling Libya's most sensitive public issues. He created a unified visual identity for both shows, balancing footage to achieve a consistent, professional look that matched each show's tone and message. Both series resonated widely with Libyan viewers and sparked meaningful discussions.

In game development, Mohamed served as Lead Developer on several notable projects. Tra Tani – Show Me Again, a puzzle game promoting the work of 24 Libyan youth artists, became the most downloaded Libyan-developed game to date. He also led the development of ASTRU, a physics-based mobile puzzle game inspired by cosmology, and Irty – My Heritage, an educational demo helping children explore Libyan history and culture through fun quizzes and interactive exploration. Across these projects, he programmed gameplay mechanics, physics, UI, and much more.

Currently, Mohamed is exploring the future of live music in digital spaces through his PhD research at the University for the Creative Arts. His work investigates how gamifying VR concerts affects the audience's experience. Mohamed’s research develops and tests different levels of gamification, aiming to uncover how these interactive elements influence audience engagement. His findings will help inform musicians, game designers, and record labels on how to design meaningful digital concert experiences in a rapidly changing industry.

Media Projects
Selected works in production and color grading
Marionette TV Series - Broadcasted on Libya Al-Ahrar TV

Role: Production Manager

Description: "Marionette" is a powerful Libyan drama series that delves into themes of power, loyalty, and moral ambiguity in a nation struggling with its future. The story follows Omar, the elusive leader of a powerful underground network, as he challenges the status quo and sparks conflict with the ruling government.

As Production Manager, I oversaw the entire production process of the 10-episode series, leading a team of over 55 cast and crew members. Despite the logistical and security complexities of filming in Libya, I successfully delivered the full series on time and within budget, completing principal photography in just 42 days. The series was broadcast on Libya Al-Ahrar TV and reached millions of viewers on air and across digital platforms, marking a significant achievement in contemporary Libyan television production.

Preview: Watch on Libya Al-Ahrar Facebook page

Masaha "Space" TV Talk Show - Broadcasted on Al-Wasat TV

Role: Colorist

Description: "Masaha" is an innovative Libyan talk show that brings together strangers from diverse backgrounds to engage in honest conversations about society, identity, and shared experiences. Through its distinctive format—participants converse without seeing each other—the show fosters empathy and understanding, often blending serious discussion with humor.

As the Colorist, I was responsible for the color correction and grading of all 15 episodes. My work ensured a cohesive visual identity that reflected the director’s creative vision. I harmonized footage from various cameras, lenses, and lighting environments, carefully balancing participants’ skin tones and wardrobe colors to create a consistent and engaging viewing experience. The result was a polished and unified look that enhanced the show's intimate and thought-provoking atmosphere.

Preview: Watch on Al-Wasat TV Youtube channel

Alash?! "Why?!" Studio Web Show - Broadcasted on Alash platform

Role: Colorist

Description: "Alash?! Studio" is a bold monthly talk show that tackled Libya’s most pressing and controversial topics, from economic policy and immigration to women’s rights and corruption. By addressing sensitive issues without hesitation, the show sparked widespread public debate and inspired meaningful conversations across social media, generating hundreds of thousands of views and shares.

As the Colorist, I crafted a consistent visual style across all 10 episodes. I unified footage from varied sources, balancing cameras, lighting setups, and host appearance to create a cohesive, polished look aligned with the director’s creative vision. This careful color work helped reinforce the show's credible, serious tone and made it visually engaging for its wide-reaching audience.

A New Reality - Best COVID-19 Awareness Video on RNW Media 2020 competition

Role: Production Manager

Description: "A New Reality" is a 5-minute awareness video that sheds light on Libya’s response to the COVID-19 pandemic, featuring heartfelt conversations with healthcare workers, everyday citizens, and key stakeholders. Produced for RNW Media’s 2020 competition, the video was honored with the Best COVID-19 Awareness Video award, recognizing its impactful storytelling and public health message.

As Production Manager, I coordinated every stage of the shoot—scheduling and facilitating interviews with multiple guests and the host, managing location logistics, and overseeing separate shooting sessions for different participants. I ensured smooth collaboration between departments, supervised on-set safety measures, and maintained production timelines. The result was a compelling narrative that called on viewers to adopt safety precautions and support one another in facing the pandemic’s challenges.

Game Development
Innovative games and interactive experiences
Irty "My Heritage"

Role: Lead Developer

Description: Irty (“My Heritage”) is an educational mobile game designed to raise awareness of Libya’s rich cultural heritage among children and adolescents. Through interactive quizzes and engaging gameplay, players explore the history, landmarks, and traditions of Libya. In the demo release, players journey through two Libyan cities, Tripoli and Shehhat, learning about their cultural significance.

As Lead Developer, I directed the development process, programming core gameplay systems and guiding the team across areas such as game physics, user interface, and audio integration. Feedback from the demo is shaping plans for expanding Irty into a full release covering more cities and further refining its educational approach.

ASTRU

Role: Lead Developer

Description: ASTRU is a mobile puzzle game that challenges players to navigate a celestial object through the complexities of space, accounting for gravitational forces from various moving bodies. Inspired by cosmological physics, the game blends scientific concepts with accessible, enjoyable gameplay.

I led the complete development lifecycle, overseeing gameplay mechanics, physics simulation, and user experience design. I coordinated and mentored a team of junior programmers to implement 60 thoughtfully crafted levels that balance challenge and fun. ASTRU was met with highly positive player feedback for its original concept and polished execution.

Tra Tani "Show me again"

Role: Lead Developer

Description: Tra Tani (“Show Me Again”) is a creative puzzle game that celebrates contemporary Libyan art. The game features artworks from 24 Libyan youth artists, embedding their work within 85 puzzle levels of increasing difficulty. Between levels, messages promoting peace and peaceful coexistence reinforce the game’s social mission as it was released in a time of active conflict in Libya.

As Lead Developer, I managed the entire development process, programming gameplay mechanics, designing the puzzles, and integrating visual art and sound. I also led the team to deliver the project efficiently on time. Tra Tani became the most downloaded Libyan-developed game to date, making a lasting impact on the local gaming and artistic communities.
